Health Monitoring

Monitor yourself for 72 hours after fire breathing. Symptoms can appear or worsen hours later.

Urgent Rule

If symptoms are severe, call 999 or go to A&E immediately

Warning Signs

Fever or high temperature

Cough or wheeze

Shortness of breath

Chest or throat heat

Coughing blood

What To Do

Mild concern: Phone NHS 24 on 111

Severe concern: Phone 999 or go to A&E

Fuel Details

Product used

Bird Brand Clear Lamp Oil / Ultra-Pure Paraffin

Chemical identity

Hydrocarbons, C10-C13, n-alkanes, <2% aromatics

Official SDS hazard

H304 — May be fatal if swallowed and enters airways

Tell Them

Time

What time did it happen

How it happened

Coughed, choked, swallowed, inhaled, or felt fuel go the wrong way

Symptoms
  • Fever or high temperature
  • Cough or wheeze
  • Shortness of breath
  • Chest or throat heat
  • Coughing blood

Emergency Actions

Burns

Cool the burn under running water for 20 minutes

Do not use ice or other substances — they can trap heat or damage the burn

Remove nearby jewellery and clothing, but do not remove anything stuck to the burn

For burns involving the face or airway, or for large, deep, or worsening burns, phone 999 or go straight to A&E

Paraffin Fire

  • Use your Fire Blanket immediately

Paraffin Spill

  • Remove ignition sources if safe
  • Keep people clear
  • Absorb Ultra-Pure Paraffin with sand
  • Collect contaminated sand for disposal

Cleaning Routine

This must be done after every Fire-Breathing session

Mouth Routine

  1. Three thorough rinses with water
  2. Alcohol-free mouthwash rinse
  3. Brush teeth for 2 minutes
  4. Water flosser (optional)

Face, Hands & General Cleaning

  1. Wash your face and hands with soap and warm water
  2. Shower to remove any Ultra-Pure Paraffin residue
  3. Put all clothing used in the wash
  4. Do not tumble dry clothing used while Fire-Breathing

Before You Leave

Remember to monitor symptoms for 72 hours

Techniques

Use the same technique for water practice and Fire Breathing.

Fire Breathing Sequence

  1. Light torch (if needed)
  2. Wet face with Dab Rag
  3. Check boundary for people and obstructions
  4. Check torch to orientate to wind behind you
  5. Big breath in, and hold
  6. Ultra-Pure Paraffin in mouth
  7. Check torch again to orient it to wind behind you
  8. Fire Breathe
  9. Clear mouth / spit and breathe normally

Technique Check

Mini O, firm corners, firm cheeks

Tongue down and clear

Head and neck neutral, abs tight

Steady consistent breath

Common Fixes

If Mist Comes Out Like A Stream:
Use less water, make the mouth opening smaller, and try again

If Mist Looks / Feels Weak:
Commit to more breath, keep it steady, and do not hesitate

If There Are Drips At The End:
Cut off the mist while your breath is still strong Do not push to the last bit of breath

If Your Mouth Shape Feels Wrong:
Reset: Mini O, firm corners, firm cheeks, tongue down and clear

If You Need To Breathe In Quickly:
Too much water / Ultra-Pure Paraffin was used Try again with less and make sure you can clear your mouth before breathing

Before Practice Checklist

Use this before Fire Breathing away from the workshop

Before Leaving

Check Met Office for the exact location

Pack Fire Blanket, Sand Spill Kit, Water, Phone, Dab Rag, Torch, and Ultra-Pure Paraffin

On Arrival

Check the weather again at the current location

Watch wind clues at head height: trees, flags, smoke, clothing, and your Torch

If wind is gusty or variable, do not Fire Breathe under any circumstances

Boundary Check

Ground is clear of holes or tripping hazards

Boundary is clear of people, clothing, items, and obstructions

No nearby combustibles, overhead hazards, electrical cables, or anything your Fire Breathing could reach

Fire Blanket, Sand Spill Kit, and Water are within reach

Before Fire Breathing

Run the Fire Breathing Sequence with water first

Water mist is clean, controlled, and travelling with the wind

Check Torch orientation to confirm the wind is behind you before Ultra-Pure Paraffin goes in your mouth
